Act as a senior character animation QA reviewer. Compare the two side-by-side clips frame by frame.

The target reproduction is on the left. The official ground-truth reference is on the right. The right reference is correct.

Your job is to identify the most important dynamic reproduction bug in the target, not to describe visual style or image quality.

Audit the clip in this order. Do not skip step 1 even if large hand or body motion differs.

1. Facial and eye animation audit:
- eye shape, eyelid pose, blink state, pupil/gaze direction, catchlight/pupil placement, and brow-eye relationship
- mouth shape and mouth-flap timing during speech
- whether the face falls into a neutral/default expression instead of the reference expression
- whether the target eyes look too wide, too round, too neutral, unfocused, or locked compared with the reference
- whether the target keeps a default eye/expression state while the reference has a sharper emotional expression

Treat eye and facial differences as animation/state bugs. Do not dismiss them as static image quality, art style, resolution, or character design differences.

2. Body and gesture animation audit:
- hand pose, finger pose, arm timing, gesture loop continuity, and follow-through
- head, hair, clothing, and secondary motion
- whether the target is merely time-shifted, or whether an animation segment/state is missing or incorrect

3. Severity decision:
- Prefer the bug that most changes the character performance and intent, not merely the largest moving object.
- If facial/eye state is wrong for most of the clip, report it even if hand timing also differs.
- If an attached motion diagnostics heatmap highlights strong missing or extra motion in any region or layer, include it in the severity decision.
- Distinguish between simple timing offset and incorrect/missing animation state.

Return a concise production QA diagnosis in this exact structure:

Eye/facial audit:
- State whether the target eyes and facial expression match the reference. Include concrete evidence and timestamps.

Motion/gesture audit:
- State whether the target hand, arm, head, hair, mouth, and gesture continuity match the reference. Include concrete evidence and timestamps.

Primary issue:
- One sentence naming the most severe bug category.

Classification:
- Choose one or more from timing offset, missing animation segment, incorrect animation state, reduced motion amplitude, eye/facial expression error, mouth/lip-sync error, gesture/pose error.
